# Product

## Register

product

## Users
Police roleplayers in a gaming community (e.g., FiveM). They use this system while "on-duty" to log their time, track incidents, and view community analytics. They require a high-contrast, efficient interface that feels like a tactical terminal.

## Product Purpose
The "POLICE ALL STAR PD" Dispatch terminal serves as a centralized hub for community management. It tracks officer duty logs, incident reports (cases), and provides leaderboard/hall-of-fame statistics to encourage activity and provide administrative oversight.

## Brand Personality
Tactical, Terminal, Precise. The interface should evoke the feeling of a modern police CAD (Computer-Aided Dispatch) system or a high-tech military terminal.

## Anti-references
Generic Bootstrap dashboards, "Standard SaaS" aesthetics (soft shadows, pastel colors), clunky legacy government software, and over-decorated "gamer" UIs that sacrifice legibility for glow.

## Design Principles
- **Terminal Efficiency**: Prioritize data density and quick-glance readability.
- **Tactical Feedback**: Use purposeful motion and high-contrast state changes to indicate status (e.g., On-Duty pulse).
- **Authoritative Hierarchy**: Information should be structured with clear weight and scale, mirroring military/police protocol.

## Accessibility & Inclusion
- High contrast for readability in dark environments.
- Clear semantic markers for status and actions.
- Respect reduced motion for terminal-style animations.
